Reaching “weirdo” developers (indie, niche, off-the-beaten-path)

Context: You want the Direct links section (and eof.news) to also reach unconventional developers — low-level tinkerers, solo indie builders, personal bloggers, IndieWeb folks, game devs, systems programmers. They often don’t show up in “top 10 tech newsletters” lists. Here’s how to discover them and get on their radar so they find your direct-links page and link back.

Where they hang out (discovery)

Where What it is How you use it
Handmade Network (handmade.network, hero.handmade.network) Forums, Discord, jams for “handmade” / low-level programmers; Wheel Reinvention Jam, Handmade Seattle Browse forums, jams, speaker lists; add people to DIRECT_LINKS; mention eof.news in a friendly way.
IndieWeb Webring (indieweb.org/directory, webring via indiewebring) Directory of people with personal sites using IndieWeb (own your data, RSS, etc.) Discover personal blogs/sites; add them to your direct list; consider joining the webring so they discover you.
indieblog.page Curated discovery for IndieWeb: 4k+ sites, random post surf, RSS; “real people, no commercial agenda” Use to find indie blogs; add good fits to DIRECT_LINKS; submit eof.news if it fits their criteria.
ooh.directory Curated directory of 2.3k+ blogs by topic (Technology, Personal, etc.); by Phil Gyford Browse Technology / niche categories; add bloggers to your list; submit eof.news blog if you have one.
BlogScroll (blogscroll.com) Open-source directory of personal sites; add via GitHub issue; has RSS for new additions Add your site; browse list for indie devs; add them to DIRECT_LINKS.
TIGSource Forums Indie game dev community (devlogs, jams, announcements) Find indie game / tooling devs with blogs; link them in Direct.
SoloDevelopment.org Solo indie devs: Discord, subreddit, marathon jams Discover solo devs and their blogs/projects; add to direct links.
Bluesky / Mastodon Fediverse: many indie and “weirdo” devs; #indieweb #handmade #zig #systems programming etc. Follow and engage; when you add someone to Direct, mention them (“Added you to our direct links”); they notice and may link back.
HN / Lobsters “Show” posts Show HN, project posts Mine for personal sites and blogs in comments/bios; add to DIRECT_LINKS.

Tactics so they find you and link back

  1. Get listed where they look — Submit eof.news (or your blog) to: indieblog.page, ooh.directory (if you have a blog), BlogScroll, IndieWeb directory. They browse these; you show up next to “weirdo” peers.

  2. Join a webring — If you have a personal/blog presence, join the IndieWeb Webring (or similar). Your site appears in the ring; others click through and may find your Direct page.

  3. Say “we link to you” where they are — When you add someone from Handmade, IndieWeb, ooh.directory, etc., post a short note on Bluesky/Mastodon or in a relevant forum: “Added [name/site] to our direct links — we link to devs directly, no platform in the middle.” Tag or mention them; they see it and may link back.

  4. Explicit “weirdo-friendly” copy on the Direct page — One line: “Indie devs, personal blogs, low-level tinkerers, game devs — we link to you. Got a blog or project? Get in touch.” Signals they’re welcome.

  5. Proactively add people from niche communities — Don’t wait for them to ask. Browse Handmade, indieblog.page, ooh.directory; add 5–10 “weirdo” devs to DIRECT_LINKS; then notify them (email or social). Many will appreciate it and link back.

  6. RSS / feed from indie directories — Some of these (e.g. indieblog.page, ooh.directory) have feeds or “recent” lists. You could (later) add a pipeline step that suggests new indie blogs to consider for DIRECT_LINKS — manual approval, then add and notify.

Summary

  • Discover: Handmade Network, IndieWeb Webring, indieblog.page, ooh.directory, BlogScroll, TIGSource, SoloDevelopment, Bluesky/Mastodon, HN/Lobsters.
  • Get discovered: Submit eof.news to those directories; join a webring; post when you add people; add “indie / weirdo welcome” copy on Direct.
  • Encourage link-back: Add them first, then tell them (“you’re on our direct links”); give them a stable permalink to share.
